Advocacy Initiatives

Self-Advocacy is the ability to understand and effectively communicate one's own needs, rights, and interests to others. It is about being the primary decision-maker in your own life, taking responsibility for making personal choices, and knowing how to ask for help or accommodations when necessary. This type of advocacy is essential for personal empowerment and self-determination, ensuring that your voice is heard in all aspects of your life, from healthcare to education and employment.

Individual Advocacy involves a person or professional speaking, acting, or writing on behalf of a single, specific person. The goal is to resolve a particular issue or injustice that the individual is facing, often when they are vulnerable or lack the power to speak up for themselves. The advocate focuses on securing the rights and entitlements of that one person, helping them navigate complex systems, and ensuring their wishes and needs are addressed by the relevant authorities or institutions.

Systems Advocacy is focused on achieving large-scale, lasting change by targeting the laws, policies, practices, and attitudes that affect an entire group of people. Instead of fixing one person's problem, advocates work to change the fundamental structures that create barriers or inequities for many. This often involves research, public campaigns, lobbying legislators, and collaborative effort among organizations to create new legislation or reform institutions, leading to broad social justice and greater inclusion.
