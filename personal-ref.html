My Personal Reflection

Learning HTML has been a lesson in patience and precision; at first, it felt like learning a cryptic new language where a single missing bracket could break an entire page. I’ve realized that while I started out just trying to memorize tags like h1 and p, the real growth happened when I began to see the "skeleton" of the web—the logic behind how information is organized for others to read. It is humbling to realize that even the simplest website requires intentional structure, and while I still find myself double-checking my syntax or struggling with file paths, there is a distinct sense of pride in seeing a blank text editor transform into a functioning webpage through my own lines of code.
